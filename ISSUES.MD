Plans for how visuals should be designed : 

"  Since Pyrite is literally known as "Fool's Gold" and naturally grows in perfect cubic crystals (which is an incredible coincidence for a voxel game!), we have the absolute perfect theme. We can also subtly mix in the official Python colors (Blue and Yellow/Gold) to pay homage to the language.

Here are highly detailed ideas for your branding assets:

1. The App Icon (Pyrite.ico / Window Icon)
This needs to be recognizable even when it is a tiny 16x16 pixel square on the Windows taskbar.

The Shape: A single, perfect 3D isometric cube.
The Material: A shiny, metallic brass/gold texture, but slightly fractured or layered (just like real raw pyrite).
The Lighting: Strong, dramatic lighting coming from the top-left. Since your engine's biggest selling point is its custom lighting system, the icon should have a gleaming bright top face, a mid-tone left face, and a deeply shadowed right face.
The Python Nod: To hint at Python without just slapping a snake on it, make the deep shadow cast by the cube a very subtle, dark Python Blue instead of pure black.
2. The Main Logo (For the Main Menu & Readme)
This is the title text combined with a visual mark.

The Mark: Instead of a single cube, the mark next to the text should be a Pyrite Cluster—three interlocking metallic cubes of different sizes jutting out of a piece of dark gray stone.
The Typography: A bold, heavy, slightly blocky font (like Impact, Bebas Neue, or a clean pixel font).
The Colors:
Make the "Py" a sleek, dark slate-blue.
Make the "rite" a bright, metallic gold.
This instantly connects "Python" and "Pyrite" visually. Give the text a sharp, dark drop-shadow to make it pop off the screen.
3. The GitHub / Project Banner (1920x1080)
This sits at the very top of your repository or project page. It should scream "Voxel Adventure" while showing off your engine's actual rendering capabilities.

The Background: Take an incredible in-game screenshot. Find a massive procedural mountain dropping into a deep ocean. Wait for the in-game sun to drop low on the horizon so your engine casts long, beautiful shadows across the greedy-meshed terrain.
The Centerpiece: The Pyrite logo perfectly centered in the sky.
The Tagline: Just underneath the logo, in a clean, elegant sans-serif font: "A High-Performance Python Voxel Engine."
The Details: Add a slight vignette (darkening the edges of the image) to draw the viewer's eye directly to the center logo.
4. The Action Label (For Save Files or Itch.io cover)
This is smaller than a banner and usually focuses on gameplay.

The Scene: First-person perspective. You are standing in a dark stone cave. Placed on the wall is a glowing block (Glowstone/Torch) that casts that warm, yellowish block-light you programmed.
The Action: In the bottom right corner, the player's 3D wooden pickaxe is raised, about to strike a block of shiny gold ore embedded in the stone.
The Vibe: It promises exploration, mining, and cozy survival.
How to actually make these?
If you want to create these yourself:

For the Icon: You can use a free voxel editor like MagicaVoxel or Blockbench. You can literally build a 16x16x16 pyrite cube, render it with shadows, and export it as a .png (which you can convert to .ico online).
For the Banner/Labels: You can use tools like Figma, GIMP, or Photoshop. Just take a beautiful screenshot from your game by pressing your ESC menu (which you already programmed to save a thumbnail!) and overlay the text.  "
